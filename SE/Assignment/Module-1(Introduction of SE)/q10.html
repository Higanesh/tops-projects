What are the types of SDLC ? -> There are several types of Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC) models, each with its own approach to organizing and executing the phases of software development. The choice of SDLC model depends on the project's requirements, complexity, and goals. Here are the most common SDLC models: 1. Waterfall Model Description: A linear and sequential approach where each phase must be completed before the next begins. Ideal for projects with well-defined requirements and low risk of changes. Phases: Requirements Design Implementation Testing Deployment Maintenance 2. Iterative Model Description: Develops the system through repeated cycles (iterations), allowing partial implementation of the system in each iteration. Suitable for projects where requirements are expected to evolve over time. Phases: Initial Planning Iterative Cycles (Requirements, Design, Implementation, Testing) Final Deployment 3. Spiral Model Description: Combines iterative development with systematic aspects of the waterfall model, focusing on risk analysis. Emphasizes early identification and mitigation of risks. Phases: Planning Risk Analysis Engineering Evaluation 4. V-Model (Verification and Validation) Description: Extension of the waterfall model that emphasizes testing at each development stage. Ensures verification and validation of each phase. Phases: Requirements Design (High-Level and Low-Level) Implementation Verification and Validation (corresponding to each design phase) Testing (Unit, Integration, System, Acceptance) 5. Agile Model Description: Focuses on iterative development, collaboration, and flexibility. Breaks down projects into small, manageable increments (sprints). Promotes continuous customer feedback and iterative refinement. Phases: Initial Planning Iterative Cycles (Planning, Designing, Building, Testing) Final Deployment 6. Scrum Model Description: A specific Agile methodology that organizes development into sprints, typically lasting 2-4 weeks. Emphasizes roles like Scrum Master, Product Owner, and Development Team. Phases: Sprint Planning Daily Scrum Meetings Sprint Development Sprint Review Sprint Retrospective
